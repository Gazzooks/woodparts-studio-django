Hardware Catalogue for Woodworking

Woodworking hardware includes the essential fasteners, fittings, and accessories that make furniture, cabinetry, and shop projects functional and durable. This catalogue highlights the most common hardware types, their uses, and practical selection tips for woodworkers.

Essential Woodworking Hardware Categories

Category Examples Common Uses
Hinges Butt, European, Piano, Concealed, Knife, Soss Cabinet doors, boxes, chests, furniture lids
Drawer Slides & Runners Side-mount, Undermount, Center, Wooden Drawers in cabinets, furniture, shop storage
Knobs & Pulls Wood, metal, ceramic, finger pulls Cabinet doors, drawers, boxes
Fasteners Wood screws, machine screws, bolts, brads, nails Assembly, joinery, mounting hardware
Connectors & Brackets Corner braces, mending plates, angle brackets, figure-8 fasteners Shelves, knock-down furniture, reinforcing joints
Surface Hardware Hasps, latches, catches, magnetic catches, locks Boxes, cabinets, doors, chests
Levelers & Casters Furniture feet, adjustable levelers, swivel casters, locking casters Tables, cabinets, mobile shop furniture
Specialty Hardware Tee nuts, threaded inserts, knock-down connectors, shelf pins, bed hardware Jigs, fixtures, modular and knock-down furniture
Plugs, Dowels & Buttons Wood plugs, dowel pins, screw hole buttons Joinery, covering screw holes, assembly
Tip: Choose hardware that matches your project's style and intended use. For heavy doors or drawers, use robust hinges and slides. For fine furniture, select hardware that complements the wood and finish.

Popular Hardware for Furniture & Cabinetmaking

  • European Concealed Hinges: Adjustable, invisible from the front, ideal for modern cabinetry.
  • Soft-Close Drawer Slides: Prevent slamming, improve longevity, and feel high-end.
  • Figure-8 Fasteners: Secure tabletops while allowing for wood movement.
  • Threaded Inserts & Tee Nuts: Provide strong, reusable threads in wood for knock-down and adjustable joints.
  • Magnetic Catches: Hold doors and lids closed without visible hardware.
  • Bed Rail Hardware: Specialized brackets for assembling/disassembling beds and large frames.

Shop Hardware & Jigs

  • Toggle Clamps: For holding workpieces in jigs and fixtures.
  • Knock-Down Connectors: Cam locks, cross dowels, and other hardware for modular shop furniture.
  • Bench Dogs & Holdfasts: Workbench accessories for securing workpieces.
  • French Cleats: For flexible wall storage and hanging cabinets.
  • Jig Hardware Kits: Pre-packaged sets of knobs, bolts, T-tracks, and accessories for building custom jigs.
