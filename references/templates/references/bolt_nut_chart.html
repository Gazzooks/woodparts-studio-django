Bolt & Nut Chart

Bolts and nuts are essential for strong, removable connections in woodworking, especially for jigs, knock-down furniture, benches, and shop fixtures. This chart covers the most common types, sizes, and practical tips for use in wood.

Common Bolt & Nut Types in Woodworking

Type Description Common Use in Woodworking
Hex Bolt Hexagonal head, full or partial thread Furniture, benches, heavy-duty joinery
Carriage Bolt Rounded head, square neck to prevent spinning Workbenches, knock-down joints, through-bolting
Lag Bolt (Lag Screw) Large wood screw with hex head, no nut needed Attaching heavy timbers, ledger boards, frames
Tee Nut Flanged nut with prongs, hammered into wood Knock-down joints, fixtures, threaded inserts
Insert Nut Threaded insert, screwed into wood Furniture, repeated assembly/disassembly
Wing Nut Hand-tightened nut with "wings" Jigs, fixtures, tool-free adjustments
Acorn Nut (Cap Nut) Dome-shaped, covers exposed bolt ends Furniture, safety, finished appearance
Tip: Use washers under bolt heads and nuts to prevent crushing wood fibers and improve joint longevity.

Practical Tips for Using Bolts & Nuts in Woodworking

  • Use a washer under both the bolt head and the nut to distribute pressure and prevent crushing wood fibers.
  • For knock-down joints, use tee nuts or insert nuts to create strong, reusable threads in wood.
  • Carriage bolts are ideal for joints where the bolt head must not spin and should sit flush with the surface.
  • Lag bolts/screws are best for heavy-duty fastening directly into wood (no nut required).
  • Use thread-locking compound or lock washers for joints subject to vibration.
