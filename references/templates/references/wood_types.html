Wood Types Reference Guide

Comprehensive guide to wood types including hardwoods, softwoods, and plywood. Use this reference to select the right wood for your project based on characteristics, workability, and intended use.

Hardwoods come from deciduous trees and are generally denser and more durable. They're ideal for furniture, flooring, and fine woodworking projects.

Plywood and engineered lumber are manufactured wood products made from wood veneers, fibers, or particles. They offer consistent properties and are available in large sheets.
Plywood Grade Guide
Grade Description
A Smooth, sanded, paintable surface with minimal defects
B Solid surface with minor defects, can be painted
C Some defects allowed, good for structural use
D Allows larger defects, structural use only
X Exterior glue (waterproof)
CDX Common construction grade with exterior glue
Marine Void-free construction with waterproof glue
