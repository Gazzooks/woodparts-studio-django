Woodworking Quick Reference Cards

Quick reference cards provide at-a-glance reminders of essential woodworking safety, measurements, tool settings, and procedures. Keep these cards handy in your shop for fast, safe, and accurate work.

Essential Safety Quick Reference

  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):
    • Wear safety glasses or a face shield at all times [1]
    • Use hearing protection when using loud tools [4]
    • Wear a dust mask or respirator when sanding or cutting [2]
    • Wear snug-fitting clothing, no jewelry, and tie back long hair [4]
    • Use gloves only for handling rough stock, never near spinning blades [6]
  • Machine Safety:
    • Use machines only if trained and authorized [2]
    • Check guards and safety devices before use [3]
    • Remove all adjusting keys and wrenches before powering on [6]
    • Inspect stock for nails, staples, and defects before cutting [2]
  • Shop Practices:
    • Keep the work area clean and free of clutter [4]
    • Plan hand positions and use push sticks/blocks as needed [5]
    • Never leave running equipment unattended [5]
    • Stand to one side of machines that may kick back [5]

Measurement & Conversion Quick Reference

  • 1 inch = 25.4 mm
  • 1 foot = 12 inches = 304.8 mm
  • 1 board foot = 144 cubic inches = 1 ft x 1 ft x 1 in
  • Common plywood sheet: 4' x 8' (1219 x 2438 mm)
  • Nominal vs. Actual Sizes: 2x4 = 1.5" x 3.5" (38 x 89 mm)
  • Fraction to Decimal: 1/8" = 0.125, 1/4" = 0.25, 3/8" = 0.375, 1/2" = 0.5, 3/4" = 0.75
For more, see: Woodworker's Pocket Reference

Tool Settings & Setup Quick Reference

  • Table Saw Blade Height: 1/8"–1/4" above the workpiece
  • Miter Saw Common Angles: 45° for corners, 22.5° for octagons
  • Drill Bit Speeds: Hardwoods: slower (1000–1500 RPM); Softwoods: faster (1500–2000 RPM)
  • Router Bit Speeds: Large bits: slower (12,000–16,000 RPM); Small bits: faster (18,000–24,000 RPM)
  • Sanding Progression: 80 → 120 → 180 → 220 grit

Emergency & First Aid Quick Reference

  • Know the location of the first aid kit and fire extinguisher
  • Shut off power immediately in case of accident
  • For serious injury: Call emergency services and apply first aid
  • Report all accidents and near-misses to your supervisor or staff
